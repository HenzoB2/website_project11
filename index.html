Air Pollution Crisis

Air pollution It's caused by factories and vehicles spreading harmful gases and fumes in the atmosphere or burning fossil fuels such as coal. It leads to respiratory problems such as asthma as well as catastrophes like global warming and holes in the ozone layer. We can reduce it by walking or biking for short trips, planting more trees to purify air and using public transport

Water Contamination

It's often caused by pouring chemicals down the sink or ship crashes (oil spills) as well as plastic waste being thrown into the sea. It's hazardous because it kills fisheries, leading to its extinction. It also spreads infection, most notably cholera to those who consume it. To stop it, we ought to use less plastic, avoid pouring oil or medicines and picking up litter near beaches and rivers.

Land Pollution

Soil pollution is caused by dumping industrial waste and overusing pesticides on farmlands. It makes land less fertile, leads to deforestation and potentially poisons groundwater. We can reduce it by following the 3-R rule which are : "Reduce, Reuse and recycle". Or by composting food scraps instead of throwing them away. Even the simplest acts like picking up rubbish and placing it in dustbins can help us protect our land.
