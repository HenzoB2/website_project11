Types of Pollution

Understanding the different forms of pollution is the first step toward creating a cleaner, healthier planet.

Air Pollution Crisis

Air pollution is caused by factories and vehicles spreading harmful gases and fumes in the atmosphere or burning fossil fuels such as coal. It leads to respiratory problems such as asthma, as well as catastrophes like global warming and holes in the ozone layer. We can reduce it by walking or biking for short trips, planting more trees to purify air, and using public transport.

Water Contamination

Water pollution is often caused by pouring chemicals down the sink or ship crashes (oil spills), as well as plastic waste being thrown into the sea. It's hazardous because it kills fish, leading to their extinction. It also spreads infection, most notably cholera, to those who consume it. To stop it, we ought to use less plastic, avoid pouring oil or medicines, and pick up litter near beaches and rivers.

Land Pollution

Land pollution is caused by dumping industrial waste and overusing pesticides on farmlands. It makes land less fertile, leads to deforestation, and potentially poisons groundwater. We can reduce it by following the 3-R rule: "Reduce, Reuse, and Recycle." Or by composting food scraps instead of throwing them away. Even the simplest acts like picking up rubbish and placing it in dustbins can help us protect our land.
